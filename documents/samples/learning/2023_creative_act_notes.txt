Key ideas:

"Creativity is not a rare ability. It is not difficult to access. Creativity is a fundamental aspect of being human."

"The best work is the work that doesn't feel like work. It feels like play."

"We're not creating for others. We're creating for ourselves, and if it resonates with others, that's a bonus."

On source material:

"We are all transmitters and receivers. We pick up signals from the universe and translate them into something others can experience."

My takeaway:

Stop trying to be creative. Just pay attention. Be present. Let things come through you.

The pressure to be original is paralyzing. Rubin says just be authentic. Originality follows naturally.

On taste:

"The ability to recognize what's good, before others do, is one of the most valuable creative skills."

Taste is developed through exposure. See lots of work. Good and bad. Taste refines.
