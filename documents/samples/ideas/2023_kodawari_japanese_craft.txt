What is kodawari?

Japanese concept: An uncompromising commitment to excellence in craft. Going far beyond "good enough."

Examples I witnessed in Japan:

- Sushi chef who spent 10 years on rice before touching fish
- Ramen shop using single recipe for 40 years, perfecting daily
- Tea ceremony masters practicing the same movements for decades
- Knife makers taking weeks on single blade

The Western contrast:

We optimize for efficiency, scale, speed. "Move fast and break things."

Kodawari optimizes for quality, depth, mastery. Slow is fine if it's excellent.

My relationships are not just "adequate." I want them deep.
